M.Sc. Thesis
Fabrication and characterization of CdZnS and Cu2ZnSnS4 (CZTS) thin films as thin-film solar cell material

CdZnS and Cu2ZnSnS4 (CZTS) have become the subject of considerable interest due to their use as thin film solar cell material. The aim of this study was to fabricate and characterize CdZnS and CZTS films. The CdZnS thin films were synthesized by Chemical Bath Deposition technique. The bath solution was composed of Cadmium chloride, zinc acetate, thiourea, ammonia and water. The concentration of the bath solution was varied as Y= 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8 where, Y= [Zn(CH3COO)2.2H2O]/{[ CdCl2.H2O] + [Zn(CH3COO)2.2H2O]}. The XRD data confirms the formation of CdZnS with wurtzite (hexagonal) structure. Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) result shows CdZnS films with a very good uniformity. The optical band gaps (Eg) of the CdZnS thin films are in the range of 2.83-2.93 eV. Resistance of the CdZnS films were found to increase as Y value was increased. CZTS thin films were deposited by sol-gel spin coating using precursor solutions prepared with copper chloride, zinc acetate, stannous chloride and thiourea in 2-methoxyehtanol followed by annealing. The XRD pattern confirms the formation of CZTS. SEM result shows CZTS film with a very porous microstructure. The samples annealed in N2 atmosphere showed better crystalline property than the sulfurized ones. N2 samples showed absorption coefficients larger than 104 cm-1 in the visible region. The optical band gaps (Eg) of the CZTS thin films were determined to be in the range of 1.467-1.531 eV.
